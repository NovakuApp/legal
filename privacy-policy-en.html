5. International Transfers

5.1 Transfers covered by adequacy / DPF

Transfers to Google, RevenueCat and Sentry (USA) are protected by the EU–US Data Privacy Framework (Commission adequacy decision of 10 July 2023) and complemented by the EU Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) 2021/914 for any data falling outside DPF scope.

5.2 Transfer to Telegram FZ-LLC (UAE) — explicit consent (Art. 49)

The UAE has no EU adequacy decision and Telegram has not signed the EU SCCs with us. Therefore, any transfer of your photograph to Telegram for moderation relies on GDPR Art. 49(1)(a) — explicit consent, which you provide on the Fan Mosaic privacy screen before any photo is uploaded. We make the following disclosures so your consent is informed:

  • The recipient is Telegram FZ-LLC, established in Dubai, United Arab Emirates.
  • UAE law may grant local authorities access rights broader than the EU's, without equivalent judicial redress in some scenarios.
  • What we transfer: a single signed URL (120 seconds TTL) pointing to your photo. The link expires automatically and is not embedded or re-shared.
  • What we do not transfer: your email, real name, team selection, or any other personal data.
  • You can withdraw consent at any time by deleting your account; future submissions will be impossible without this consent.

5.3 Transfers via social media platforms

Although the EEA controller for Instagram (Meta Platforms Ireland Limited) and TikTok (TikTok Technology Limited) are both Irish entities, content we publish to those platforms is replicated to their global infrastructure, including locations outside the EEA. The platforms describe these transfers in their own policies; for transparency we summarise the position below:

  • Instagram (Meta). Meta operates a global infrastructure with primary hubs in the EU (Ireland, Sweden, Denmark), the United States and Asia. Transfers from the EU are protected by the EU–US Data Privacy Framework (Commission adequacy decision of 10 July 2023) for US transfers, and by the EU Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) 2021/914 for any other transfers.
  • TikTok. TikTok's privacy policy (as of 2026) states that EEA user data is stored "in servers located in the United States, Malaysia and Singapore" and that TikTok relies on a combination of adequacy decisions, SCCs and GDPR Art. 49 derogations to lawfully transfer data outside the EEA. TikTok has also announced "Project Clover", a European data-localisation initiative with data centres in Norway, Ireland and (under construction) Finland.

Material transparency disclosure. On 2 May 2025 the Irish Data Protection Commission fined TikTok €530 million for failing to adequately protect EEA user data from access by personnel in the People's Republic of China (Decision IN-21-9-1). TikTok has stated it disagrees with the decision and is appealing. We mention this here so that, before consenting to social media distribution under §B.9, you can make an informed choice taking into account this regulatory finding. You may opt out of social media inclusion specifically (see §B.9.5) while remaining a participant in the in-app mosaic.

By accepting this Privacy Policy at v3.0.0, you acknowledge these transfers occur for content you have previously consented to make public via §B.3, and to publish externally via §B.9.

7. Your Rights

  • Access (Art. 15) — request a copy of your data.
  • Rectification (Art. 16) — fix inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Erasure (Art. 17) — delete your account directly from Profile → Delete Account, or email us.
  • Restriction (Art. 18) — temporarily freeze processing while a complaint is reviewed.
  • Portability (Art. 20) — receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Objection (Art. 21) — object to processing based on legitimate interest (Sentry, fraud prevention).
  • Withdraw consent (Art. 7(3)) — at any time, without affecting prior lawful processing.

To exercise any right, email novakuapp@gmail.com. We respond within 30 days (extendable by 60 days for complex requests, Art. 12(3)).

13. Security Measures

  • Transport: TLS 1.2+ for all client-server traffic.
  • Storage: AES-256 at rest (Supabase Storage + Postgres).
  • Authentication: Google OAuth 2.0 + Supabase JWTs with rotating refresh tokens.
  • Access control: Row-Level Security (RLS) on every table; the client cannot read or modify other users' rows.
  • Server-side immutability of consent record: the four privacy_* columns are protected by a database trigger that rejects any attempt to overwrite a stamped value.
  • Moderation surface: moderators see only a 120-second signed URL — no email, no full name, no IP.
  • Logging: all destructive endpoints (delete-account, photo replacement) are audit-logged server-side.

14. Cookies and On-Device Storage

Our apps do not use HTTP cookies (no web context). On-device, we use:

  • Android Keystore + flutter_secure_storage for sensitive identifiers (user ID, email, Pro entitlement cache).
  • SharedPreferences for non-sensitive UI state and the pre-auth consent record awaiting sync.
  • Google AdMob + UMP SDKs, which store consent tokens locally to suppress re-prompts on every launch.
  • Sentry SDK, which buffers crash events offline until the next online session.

Section B. Fan Mosaic

B.1 Why Fan Mosaic has extra privacy considerations

Fan Mosaic publishes your photograph in your team's public mosaic. A photograph of an identifiable person is personal data under GDPR. It is NOT biometric data in our context — we do not perform any "specific technical processing" for unique identification (Recital 51 + EDPB 3/2019), so no Art. 9 special-category regime applies. The legal basis is your explicit consent under Art. 6(1)(a), recorded on the privacy screen.

B.2 Additional data collected

  • The photograph itself (stored in Supabase Storage, Frankfurt).
  • The team you chose + your custom display name.
  • Approval status (pending, approved, rejected) and rejection reason if any.
  • Submission timestamp + first approval timestamp (for sports-fair tiebreaker in ranking).
  • Proof-of-consent (Art. 7.1): the four privacy_* columns described below.

B.3 Public visibility

Once approved, your photo is part of a publicly visible mosaic inside the app and may appear in any screenshot or share generated by any user. By accepting the privacy screen you acknowledge this public visibility.

B.4 Photo moderation (Telegram, UAE)

Before publication, every photo is reviewed by a human moderator over a private Telegram channel operated by Novaku.

  • The moderator receives only a 120-second signed URL — not the file itself.
  • The moderator sees no account-identifying data alongside the photo.
  • Telegram is operated by Telegram FZ-LLC, Dubai, United Arab Emirates. The transfer relies on GDPR Art. 49(1)(a) — explicit consent — described in §5.2 above.
  • The moderation message persists in the private channel for at most 90 days, after which it is purged.

B.5 Proof of consent — the four privacy_* columns

When you accept the privacy screen, your client stamps the following onto your wc_fans row at first sign-in. These columns are immutable by database trigger and constitute our Art. 7.1 evidence that you consented.

  • privacy_accepted_at — UTC timestamp of acceptance.
  • privacy_policy_version — the semantic version of this document.
  • privacy_policy_hash — SHA-256 of the canonical English text shown.
  • privacy_consent_locale — the language you actually read (en, es, fr, de, pt, ar).

B.6 Permissions requested

  • INTERNET — always.
  • CAMERA — only when you tap "Take photo".
  • READ_MEDIA_IMAGES (Android 13+) / READ_EXTERNAL_STORAGE — only when you tap "Choose from gallery".
  • com.google.android.gms.permission.AD_ID — auto-granted, required by AdMob ≥ 21.

B.7 Ads (AdMob + UMP)

The free tier displays Google AdMob ads. On first launch in the EEA/UK/CH, the Google User Messaging Platform (UMP) shows you a consent dialog that lets you choose between personalised and non-personalised ads. You can re-open this dialog at any time from Profile → Privacy options. If you upgrade to Pro, no ads are shown.

B.8 Mosaic regeneration after deletion

When you delete your account, your photo is removed from storage immediately. However, composite mosaic JPEGs already rendered cannot be retroactively edited; they are replaced at the next 02:00 UTC daily regeneration. Within 24 hours of deletion your photo no longer appears in any new mosaic.

B.9 Public distribution on social media (Instagram & TikTok)

Beyond the in-app public visibility of your photo as part of the mosaic (§B.3), we publish rendered mosaic content and related promotional materials on our official social media accounts — Instagram and TikTok, both @novakuapp. The processing is detailed below.

B.9.1 What we publish

  • Rendered country flag mosaics — your photo may appear as one tile within a mosaic.
  • Milestone announcement cards (e.g., "Spain just completed its mosaic — 100 real fans").
  • Matchday preview cards listing two competing countries with their aggregate fan counts.
  • Country leaderboards and other aggregate-data promotional images.
  • Accompanying captions and hashtags — these do not include your account name, real name, email or any other personal identifier we hold about you.

We do not publish: individual user photos as standalone posts, your name, your account, your email, your IP, your location, or any other personal identifier outside the visual mosaic context.

B.9.2 Frequency

Automated daily posts orchestrated by our publishing system, typically 1 to 3 posts per day during active tournament windows (e.g., the FIFA World Cup 2026, 11 June – 19 July 2026) and lower cadence outside tournament windows. All posts are scheduled and reviewed by a human before publication.

B.9.3 Legal basis & balancing test (Art. 6(1)(f) + EDPB Guidelines 1/2024)

Our legal basis is legitimate interest pursuant to GDPR Article 6(1)(f), assessed against the three-part test reaffirmed by the EDPB Guidelines 1/2024:

  • Purpose test. Promotion of the Service and engagement with the football fan community is a clearly identified, lawful and present commercial interest of Novaku as an independent developer.
  • Necessity test. Publication of the rendered mosaic is necessary to demonstrate the social proof element of the product ("a flag built from real fans"). A redacted or synthetic alternative would fail to communicate the product value proposition and would mislead potential users.
  • Balancing test. We weigh our interest against your rights and reasonable expectations:
    • You have already given explicit consent (Art. 6(1)(a)) to the photo being a publicly visible component of the mosaic inside the app (§B.3) — your reasonable expectation already includes public visibility.
    • We transmit no personally identifying metadata alongside the visual content (no name, email, account, IP).
    • You retain an unconditional right to object (Art. 21) that we honour without requiring you to leave the in-app mosaic (see §B.9.5).
    • The impact on you is limited to additional channels of distribution of content you already consented to publish.
    We conclude the balance favours processing. A full Legitimate Interest Assessment (LIA) is available on request to novakuapp@gmail.com.

B.9.4 Withdrawing your photo from the mosaic

If you delete your photo via the Service (Profile → Delete Account, see §7), your photo will be removed from the in-app mosaic and excluded from all future social media publications within the next regeneration cycle.

Past posts cannot be retracted by us. Once a mosaic image has been published to Instagram or TikTok, those platforms process it as independent controllers and we have no technical ability to unilaterally remove the post in a way that retroactively removes your specific tile from a composite image already in circulation. We commit to the following on takedown requests:

  • We will delete or hide the entire social media post containing your photo within 7 days of receiving a written takedown request at novakuapp@gmail.com.
  • We will forward your request to the relevant platform's privacy team where applicable and provide you with reference numbers.
  • Once deleted by us, the platform may still retain a copy in backups for a limited period (typically 30–90 days) per its own policies. The post will no longer be visible to the public.

B.9.5 Right to object — opt-out specifically from social media

You have an absolute right to object (Art. 21 GDPR) to your photo being included in social media publications, even while remaining a participant in the in-app mosaic. To exercise this right:

  • Email novakuapp@gmail.com with subject prefix "Social opt-out" and your registered email address.
  • We will set an excluded_from_social flag on your wc_fans row within 7 days. From that moment your photo is excluded from all future renders intended for social media distribution; your photo remains in the in-app mosaic.
  • We will confirm in writing within the same 7-day window.
  • You may reverse this at any time by writing again.

Exercising this opt-out does not require you to delete your account or leave the in-app mosaic, and incurs no consequence on your access to the Service.

B.9.6 Re-consent for v3.0.0

v3.0.0 introduces social media distribution as a new processing scope not contemplated by v2.0.0. All users who accepted v2.0.0 will be presented with an in-app re-consent dialog on next launch. You have three options:

  1. Accept v3.0.0 — your participation continues with social media distribution included.
  2. Accept v3.0.0 with social opt-out — your participation continues without your photo being used in social media publications (§B.9.5 flag is set automatically).
  3. Decline v3.0.0 — your account remains valid but your photo is removed from the next mosaic regeneration (effectively the same as §7 erasure for the photo). Other data is retained under the v2.0.0 terms you previously accepted.

Your choice is recorded in an immutable database column (privacy_v3_choice) alongside the existing privacy_* proof-of-consent columns described in §B.5.
